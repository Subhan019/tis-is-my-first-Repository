About Us

Zala Hospital — A Beacon of Health in Afghanistan

Founded in 2005 in the heart of Kabul, Zala Hospital has grown to become one of Afghanistan's most trusted and respected healthcare institutions. With over two decades of service, we have dedicated ourselves to delivering compassionate, evidence-based medical care that meets international standards — while remaining deeply rooted in Afghan culture and values.

Our 350-bed facility houses 18 specialized departments, modern diagnostic labs, a fully equipped ICU, and a 24-hour emergency unit — providing comprehensive healthcare for families across Kabul and beyond.

✅
International Standards Accredited by Afghanistan's Ministry of Public Health and aligned with WHO protocols.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your visit to Zala Hospital.

You can book an appointment online using the form on this page, call our reception at +93 700 100 200, or visit the hospital registration desk in person at our Kabul location. We aim to confirm all bookings within 2 hours during working hours.
Yes. Zala Hospital welcomes patients from across Afghanistan and internationally. We have translators available for Dari, Pashto, Uzbek, Arabic, and English. International patients should bring their passport and insurance documents.
Please bring your Tazkira (national ID card) or passport, any previous medical reports or test results, a list of current medications, and your insurance card if applicable. For children, bring the child's vaccination booklet and guardian ID.
Absolutely. Our Emergency Department operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year — including Fridays and all public holidays. Call +93 700 999 911 for our ambulance service. No appointment is needed for emergencies.
We accept cash (Afghani and USD), M-Paisa mobile payments, major insurance plans (ANA, government employee), and NGO/humanitarian referral letters. For financially vulnerable patients, our social welfare desk can discuss subsidized care options.
Yes, Zala Hospital launched a telemedicine programme in 2022. Video consultations are available for follow-up appointments and non-emergency cases. Contact reception to schedule a telemedicine session. Home visits are available for elderly or mobility-impaired patients within Kabul city limits.
Lab results are typically ready within 4–24 hours depending on the test type. You can collect results in person from the Lab Reception, or ask your doctor to include them in your patient portal record. For urgent results, our lab team contacts the ordering physician directly.
Yes, Zala Hospital has a fully stocked 24-hour pharmacy on the ground floor, adjacent to the main emergency entrance. It carries prescription medications, over-the-counter drugs, medical supplies, and baby care products. Discounts are available for admitted patients.

Important: When to Call Emergency

Call immediately for: chest pain, difficulty breathing, loss of consciousness, severe bleeding, stroke symptoms (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty), severe burns, poisoning, or any life-threatening condition. Do not drive yourself — call our ambulance.
